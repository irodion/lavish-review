# The cockpit is a guided change narration surface; the Review Step replaces the Claim as the spine

The layered cockpit ([ADR-0009](./0009-layered-claim-evidence-cockpit.md)) made the Claim its L2 spine — "the assertions the reviewer must judge": behavior changes, risks with challenge questions, suspicious omissions, verify steps. In practice that vocabulary pulls every review toward *issue-finding*. The cockpit presents risks, omissions, and verification tasks and asks the human to adjudicate them — territory already crowded with AI review tools, and not the job this skill exists for. Dogfooding confirmed it: the tool judged the change instead of guiding the reviewer through it. The reviewer's scarcest resource on a large branch is *understanding* — where behavior changed, where code was only refactored, how the tests relate, and in what order to read — not a queue of AI-authored findings to accept or reject.

**Decision.** The cockpit is reframed from a claim/evidence adjudication surface into a **guided change narration surface**. The L2 spine changes from **Claim** to **Review Step**: one guided stop on the walkthrough — what changed here, what behavior impact it has, why it sits at this point on the route, what the human should compare, and the exact evidence hunk it lands on. A Review Step is *not* a finding. The layer model (L0 orientation, L1 threads, L2 steps, L3 evidence), the Review Route as thread order, and the hybrid dynamics of [ADR-0009](./0009-layered-claim-evidence-cockpit.md) are unchanged; only the substrate that fills L2 changes.

**Decision (structural reuse).** A Review Step keeps the Claim's structural skeleton — a stable run-scoped id, `summary`/`detail`, per-step analyst confidence ([ADR-0012](./0012-reviewer-state-and-verdict-line.md)), evidence refs with hunk anchors ([ADR-0014](./0014-deck-presentation-mode.md)), live-evidence seams, and the step-scoped ask affordance ([ADR-0015](./0015-claim-scoped-questions.md)) — so Deck Mode, the Cockpit Linter, the disposition bridge, and step-scoped questions port with a rename, not a rebuild. What changes is the *content contract*, carried by `review-analysis/0.4`: `kind` becomes required **`impact`**, `challenge_questions` become **`review_prompts`**, and each step gains a required **`why_now`** (one sentence justifying its route position) and optional **`relates_to`** (step ids, integrity-checked — the way test steps link the behavior they document). Step ids are `t<N>.s<M>`.

**Decision (Behavior Impact).** Every step carries one impact from a closed vocabulary:

- `behavior-change` — user-visible, API-visible, runtime, config, persistence, error-handling, security, or performance behavior changed.
- `behavior-preserving` — refactor, relocation, extraction, naming, or internal simplification that *appears intended* to preserve behavior.
- `test-change` — tests added, removed, or re-aimed, with the behavior they document.
- `mechanical-change` — generated files, lockfiles, vendored code, formatting, build metadata.
- `unknown-impact` — the narrator cannot honestly tell without more context, and must state what context is missing.

Impact lives on **steps only**. A thread's character is *derived* — computed at render time as impact counts over its steps ("3 behavior-change · 1 test · 5 preserving") — never authored, so a mixed thread looks mixed and a behavior change smuggled into a refactor thread is never masked by a thread badge.

**Decision (the preserving-label is the expensive one).** A step wrongly labeled `behavior-change` costs the reviewer a little wasted attention; a step wrongly labeled `behavior-preserving` invites them to skim past a real change — the failure that destroys the label's trust and with it the product. The asymmetry is defended on three sides: the narrator prompt makes `behavior-preserving` a label that must be *earned* (state what is preserved and what to compare; when in doubt, `unknown-impact`); the schema requires a preservation-check `review_prompt` and ≥1 evidence ref on every preserving step; and the render styles it as *verify-then-skim*, not "safe" — under the judgment-color discipline (no green, no checkmark), with analyst confidence shown prominently. The narrator classifies likely intent and exposes uncertainty; it never proves equivalence (out of scope, and unprovable from a diff).

**Decision (intent may narrate, but only evidence may classify).** This is the load-bearing rule. The "why" a reviewer needs has two layers with different trust requirements. *Purpose* — why a thread exists — may be narrated from attributed **Goal Evidence** ([ADR-0010](./0010-goal-evidence-ingestion.md)): "the goal says X; this thread appears to deliver it by…", with the attribution visible. *Impact* — did behavior change here — must derive from the diff and bounded widening **alone**. A commit message claiming "pure refactor, no behavior change" is quotable testimony to *check* ("the commits call this a pure refactor"), never a classification input. The narrator stays warm; the labels stay cold. [ADR-0011](./0011-independent-analysis-context.md) blindness survives verbatim — the analyst is now an independent change *narrator*, but it still never sees the authoring conversation, and the orchestrator still never edits its testimony.

**Decision (narrate by default; hunting is lens-gated).** Default analysis narrates. The only default **Attention Notes** are two, and both are narration, not adjudication: an *untested behavior change* (a muted note on its step — the test-linkage story told from the negative side) and *goal-unserved work* (at L0 alignment, unchanged). Attention Notes are plain `{text, evidence?}` — no severity, no category, no prompt, no disposition of their own; they never appear in the Map and are never counted in progress. Everything else — risk categories and levels, security/performance/concurrency checklists, Language Lens risk hunting, verify checklists — returns only through an opt-in **Focus Lens**. The default Test Checklist is retired: "run this command to check" is issue-finder posture.

**Decision (the deliverable is still the human's review).** The session still ends in the human's account, not a comprehension log ([ADR-0012](./0012-reviewer-state-and-verdict-line.md) survives). Per-step judgment moves to a five-state vocabulary — `unreviewed | looks-right | concern | follow-up | skipped` — where `looks-right` replaces `verified` (attesting comprehension + no objection, honest now that verify-claims are gone), `follow-up` replaces `question-open`, and `skipped` is new: a *deliberate, attributed* act distinct from `unreviewed` (absence), so the baked account reports honest coverage rather than an unfinished review. One axis, four active states — no separate understood×approve axes. The bake leads with concerns, then follow-ups, then coverage (looks-right / skipped-with-impacts / unreviewed — listed, never hidden).

**Decision (clean break).** `review-analysis/0.4` replaces `0.3` outright; the validator accepts only 0.4, with no dual-schema code and no `kind`→`impact` mapping table. An open session whose recorded analysis predates the current schema resolves to *stale — regenerate* (resume-anyway not offered, since the loop and bake can no longer speak that session's language); already-baked cockpits are self-contained ([ADR-0013](./0013-self-contained-cross-platform-packaging.md)) and need no code support. The term **Review Route** is kept; "Narration Route" is not introduced.

## Consequences

- `analysis.json` moves to `review-analysis/0.4`: `threads[].claims[]` becomes `threads[].steps[]` with `impact`, `why_now`, `review_prompts` (conditionally required — ≥1 on `behavior-change`, `behavior-preserving`, `unknown-impact`; optional on `test-change`, `mechanical-change`), optional `relates_to`, and optional `attention_notes[]`. `alignment`, `widened_into`, `test_runner`, and `diagrams` are unchanged. The validator additionally rejects an authored thread-level `impact`.
- The isolated analyst ([ADR-0011](./0011-independent-analysis-context.md)) is rewritten from "independent reviewer who forms claims" to "independent change narrator who forms a guided route." Most of the reframe lives in that prompt; the schema guarantees floors (presence, integrity, closed vocabularies), not narration quality.
- Deck Mode ([ADR-0014](./0014-deck-presentation-mode.md)) becomes the *default* on a served review (document mode one toggle away; `file://` stays document-only), and the route's first stop is L0 staged as an orientation card. No new narration chrome — the Stage rendering one step *is* the narration.
- The disposition bridge ([ADR-0012](./0012-reviewer-state-and-verdict-line.md)), the Q&A Bake, and the Session Evaluator learn the new vocabulary and the schema-mismatch rule. Step-scoped questions ([ADR-0015](./0015-claim-scoped-questions.md)) are unchanged but for `claim`→`step` in the payload.
- The Change Classifier's nothing-hidden invariant is untouched: every changed file remains reachable at L3, and mechanical steps stay on the route rather than being dropped.
- The Escape Boundary ([ADR-0002](./0002-deterministic-escape-boundary.md)) becomes more load-bearing, not less: narration prose, `why_now`, review prompts, attention notes, and quoted Goal Evidence are all untrusted text crossing the same boundary as before.
- The Focus Lens catalog (#31–#34) stays paused; this ADR fixes the seam it re-attaches to — lenses re-weight the narration and may add hunting, they never become the default spine.
